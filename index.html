Simpósio Brasileiro de Automação Inteligente

O Simpósio Brasileiro de Automação Inteligente (SBAI) é o principal evento científico em Automação Inteligente na América Latina, sendo organizado/patrocinado pela Sociedade Brasileira de Automática (SBA). O evento é uma oportunidade única de fazer contatos, construir parcerias e trocar ideias com importantes atores da área de Sistemas Automação Inteligente, além de uma variedade de pesquisadores experientes, desenvolvedores e profissionais.

Seguindo a tradição, o evento tem foco na temática de Sistemas e Automação Inteligentes, com interesse em assuntos atuais como IoT, automação, robótica, otimização, instrumentação e inteligência computacional. O interesse é oferecer importantes discussões e contribuições teóricas e, também, em aplicações de interesse das diferentes indústrias do Brasil e da América Latina.

O SBAI 2021 -- XV Simpósio Brasileiro de Automação Inteligente, acontecerá na Universidade Federal do RIo Grande -- FURG, no município de Rio Grande / RS.

ORGANIZAÇÃO

SILVIA SILVA DA COSTA BOTELHO

Universidade Federal do Rio Grande – FURG

D.Sc. in Robotic and AI at LAAS/CNRS under supervisor of D.Sc Rachid Alami, Toulouse, France. Her doctoral thesis was focused on multi-robots systems. She graduated in Electric Engineering in 1992 and Master in Computer Science in 1996 at the Federal University of Rio Grande do SUl (UFRGS), Porto Alegre, Brazil. Her main research interests are robotics, computer vision, image processing, pattern recognition and machine learning. Currently, she is Full Professor and Director of the Center for Computational Science - C3 at Federal University of Rio Grande - FURG, and Director of the iTec/FURG EMBRAPII Unit in Robotic System and Automation. She is a member of the advisory committee on Technological Development of the National Research being a CNPq Research Fellow of Productivity in Technological Development and Innovative Extension (DT 1D). In 2020, Professor Silvia won the Rio Grande do Sul Researcher Award for Artificial Intelligence.

Paulo Lilles Jorge Drews Junior

Universidade Federal do Rio Grande – FURG

D.Sc. and M.Sc. in Computer Science at Federal University of Minas Gerais(UFMG) under supervisor of Prof. Mario Campos, Belo Horizonte, Brazil. His doctoral dissertation was focused in restoration of images acquired in participating media. His master thesis was focused on change detection and shape retrieval in 3D Maps. He graduated in Computer Engineering in 2007 at the Federal University of Rio Grande (FURG), Rio Grande, Brazil. His main research interests are robotics, computer vision, image processing, pattern recognition and machine learning. He was a researcher at the ISR Coimbra's Mobile Robotics Lab, collaborating, as a research fellow on the FP projects Prometheus and IRPS. He was also a visiting researcher in the Autonomous System Lab at QCAT-CSIRO, Brisbane, Australia. Currently, he is Assistant Professor of the Center for Computational Science - C3 at Federal University of Rio Grande - FURG, and Planning Coordinator of the iTec/FURG EMBRAPII Unit in Robotic System and Automation. He is also a CNPq Research Fellow of Productivity in Technological Development and Innovative Extension (DT 2).

Vinícius Menezes de Oliveira

Universidade Federal do Rio Grande – FURG

D. Sc. Control and Automation at PPGEE/UFRGS (2008) under supervisor of Prof. Walter Fetter Lages. His doctoral dissertation was focused on nonlinear predictive control of brachiation robots. M.
